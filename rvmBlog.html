Reverse Vending Machine

A Reverse Vending Machine (RVM) is a machine that takes in empty and used plastic bottles and returns money to the user. RVM is planned to play a revolutionary role in the fight against climate change caused by plastic waste. Reverse Vending Machines were introduced because it is hard to pick out plastic from a pile of trash and recycle or use it for beneficial purposes.

Here, at Smart City Lab NCAI, a similar concept was implemented for environmental sustainability. The team developed a complete RVM system by working on the backend engineering of a typical vending machine. Complex AI algorithms were used to design and develop the RVM currently placed at NED University.

The team at Smart City Lab NCAI collaborated with different organizations and businesses on the RVM project. Instead of giving money to people for the bottle they put in, the RVM designed by Smart city lab gives coupons and discounts on various products. These products include fast-food chains, clothing brands, and so much more. The possibilities of the types of discounts that can be given with the help of these RVM are endless. Smart City Lab is always looking to collaborate with different businesses that want to increase their popularity and sales among citizens while raising awareness on environmental sustainability.
